The Problem

DYPER had launched REDYPER‚Ñ¢ nationally via mail-back in 2020. The next phase ‚Äî weekly curbside pickup at scale ‚Äî demanded an entirely new Enterprise Gig-Aggregation layer. No infrastructure existed. No playbook. No precedent. Series B depended on proving it could work across 25+ markets.

Build the Pickup Layer from Zero

DYPER's mail-back model hit its ceiling. Scaling to weekly curbside pickup across 25+ metro areas required a full gig-aggregation engine ‚Äî driver networks, real-time dispatch, regional aggregation infrastructure, multi-jurisdictional compliance, and a tech platform to tie it all together. None of it existed. Brandon Lee joined in July 2021 to build it.

No Unified Infrastructure

No layer to bridge DYPER subscriber data into Recyclops operations. Billing, routing, comms, and subscriber sync all ran through manual CSV exports ‚Äî fragile, slow, and unscalable.

25+ Regulatory Environments

Each metro required distinct waste permits, carrier licensing, and compliance standards. No framework existed. Multi-state gig-labor laws added another layer of ambiguity at national scale.

Manual Pilot, No Repeatable Process

The pilot ran on CSV exports, scripted geocoding, and manual deduplication. No Go/No-Go gates, no dependency maps, no standardized launch playbook ‚Äî impossible to scale without complete rebuilding.

Runway Pressure & Cost Ceiling

Series A constraints meant every inefficiency had a dollar cost. Manual route planning was expensive and slow. No market readiness scoring existed. Cost-per-market-launch was unsustainably high.
